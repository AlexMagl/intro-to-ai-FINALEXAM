LEPL Ilia State University GPA Calculation Rules

1. At Ilia State University, the GPA (Grade Point Average) represents the quotient of the sum of the products of (the GP (Grade Point) earned within each course) and (the credits obtained for each course), divided by the sum of the credits of the courses completed by the student.
GPA = Σ (GP of each course × Credits of each course) / Σ (Sum of credits of each course)

2. GP (Grade Point) represents the converted score of the grade received within one course, on a 4-point scale.

3. According to the GP system, the grading scale is divided into the following categories:

  • F - 0-50 points (0 points, Fail)
  • E - 51-60 points
  • D - 61-70 points
  • C - 71-80 points
  • B - 81-90 points
  • A - 91-95 points
  • A+ - 96-100 points

4. According to the GP system, within each course, a grade in category E (51-60) is unchanged and equals 1.00 GP; grades in categories D, C, B, and A (from 61 up to and including 95 points) increase equally in increments of 1/12 (≈0.08); a grade in category A+ (from 96 up to and including 100 points) is unchanged and equals 4.00 GP.

5. GPA is awarded at Ilia State University within all academic programs, except for the doctoral level of study. GPA is also not awarded for certificate programs.

6. At Ilia State University, only the final GPA is issued, which is reflected in the diploma supplement. This does not apply to the one-tier program of Certified Physician (M.D.).

7. When calculating the final GPA, fractional values are rounded to the nearest tenth.
